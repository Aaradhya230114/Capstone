FAN

A fan is a machine that moves air using rotating blades, typically powered by an electric motor, to provide cooling, ventilation, or other industrial purposes. These devices, which can range from personal table fans to large ceiling fans, work by creating an airflow that circulates air, cools a room, or exhausts heat and fumes. Fans are used in homes, offices, and various industrial applications for comfort, ventilation, and drying, and they are generally more energy-efficient than air conditioning units. Function: Fans create airflow by using rotating blades, also called an impeller, to push air. Power Source: Most modern fans use an electric motor, but some can be powered by other sources like handcranks or internal combustion engines. Applications: Comfort: Circulating air in rooms to provide a cooling sensation. Ventilation: Exhausting stale or noxious air, dust, and fumes from enclosed areas. Cooling: Cooling motors, transmissions, and other products. Other uses: Drying materials, conveying light materials, and providing a draft for fires. Types: Common types include ceiling fans, table fans, and pedestal fans, each designed for different needs. HEATER

A heater is a device that heats something, most commonly used to warm up a room, a car, or water. It can also refer to a person who heats something, an element in an electron tube, or, in slang, a powerful fastball in baseball or a pistol.
